A woodcut printed in black ink on handmade Indian paper, Zarina's Dividing Line presents a thick, black, meandering line incising a pale-coloured surface into haphazard parts. This line is no ordinary line. It is contentious, elusive and dislocated, with no clear beginning or end. While it seems to originate somewhere out of frame, beyond the confines of its paper surface, any presumption of roots lying elsewhere remains speculative at best. Also speculative is the nature of this line's intent. Must it be, as its title suggests, a line dividing? Or, could it be, just as emphatically, a line reconciling?
